# Trading Learnings (Graduated Rules)

Rules below have been observed 3+ times in daily_learning.json and are confirmed patterns.
Read these BEFORE making any prediction. Never violate a graduated rule unless market regime has fundamentally changed.

- Do NOT call market direction in the first 15-30 minutes on gap-up days. Wait for price structure to form before determining if it's bullish, bearish, or sideways. Gap-up opens can: (1) reject and reverse, (2) consolidate then continue, (3) consolidate then break opposite direction. The opening volatility is NOT predictive of session direction.
  Context: Observed 4 times (2026-06-30, 2026-07-02, 2026-07-03, 2026-07-06). Gap-ups create opening range volatility that can look bullish, bearish, or sideways in the first 15 min but resolve differently. On 2026-07-06, called sideways at 09:45 based on 7-bar consolidation, but market was bullish (continuous higher lows from bar 1 = bullish flag, not consolidation). On 2026-07-02, gap-up with higher highs was correctly bullish but timing was wrong (needed to wait for pullback). Wait for structure: if making higher lows = bullish flag, if making lower highs = bearish flag, if oscillating without clear HH/HL or LH/LL = sideways.

- Do NOT call sideways when price is making Higher Highs AND Higher Lows (HH/HL pattern). That is a bull trend with consolidation pauses, NOT a sideways range. True sideways requires oscillating structure with BOTH higher/lower highs AND higher/lower lows alternating. If price is making consistent higher lows = bullish continuation, even if it pauses or consolidates temporarily. Wait for actual reversal structure (lower highs + lower lows) before calling sideways.
  Context: Observed 3 times (2026-06-30, 2026-07-06, 2026-07-13). Repeatedly misidentified bull trend continuation as sideways consolidation. On 2026-06-30, slow grind with consecutive higher lows over 60+ min led to breakout (should have recognized as bull accumulation, not range). On 2026-07-06, gap-up with continuous higher lows from bar 1 was a bullish flag, not consolidation. On 2026-07-13, after gap-down recovery with HH/HL structure (24,000→24,129→24,157→24,162→24,260), called sideways at "double top" 24,162 but market broke out bullish to 24,260. A consolidation pause within an uptrend (e.g., double-top test) is NOT the same as a sideways range — it's a bull continuation pattern until price makes lower lows.
